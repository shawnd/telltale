Why Vitamin D

Vitamin D is a critical nutrient for human beings. You can get it from the sun, food, or supplements.

It is associated with a wide range of benefits, including increased cognition, immune health, bone health and well-being. Supplementation can also reduce the risks of cancer, heart disease, diabetes and multiple sclerosis.

Who needs Vitamin D?

People at risk for deficiency include, those who do not get enough sunlight, people with darker skin, the elderly and infants.

There is only a sufficient amount of UV light coming from the sun when the UV index is 3 or higher, which only occurs year-round near the equator, between the 37th parallels.

Complications of Deficiency

Complications of Vitamin D deficiency include: increased risk of heart disease, cognitive impairment, asthma in children, cancer, rickets, bone disease.
